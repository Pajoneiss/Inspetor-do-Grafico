## 1. Dow Theory: The Physics of the Market
Created by Charles Dow (founder of the Wall Street Journal and creator of the Dow Jones index) in the late 19th century, this theory is to the market what the Law of Gravity is to physics. You may not like it, but it will act upon you.

### The 6 Immutable Laws Explained

#### LAW 1: The Market Discounts Everything
This is the hardest rule to accept. It states that **everything** that is knowable about an asset is already in the price.

*   **The Concept:** Fears of war, profit expectations, backstage gossip... millions of people analyzing this and buying or selling instantly adjust the price to the "fair" value in that millisecond.
*   **The Beginner's Trap:** The trader reads news: "Amazon profit breaks record!". He rushes to buy. The price falls. Why? Because the "Big Players" knew this weeks ago and had already bought. When the news comes out, they sell to realize profit on top of the sardine who arrived late ("Buy the rumor, sell the news").
*   **Bot Conclusion:** The Inspector doesn't read the newspaper. He reads the price. If the news is good but price falls, the truth is the fall.

#### LAW 2: The Market Has Three Trends (The Ocean Metaphor)
Imagine you are at the beach. The sea has complex movements, but Dow organized everything into three scales. Understanding which scale you are trading is vital.

1.  **The Tide (Primary Trend):**
    *   *Duration:* Years (1 to 5+ years).
    *   *What it is:* The secular Bull Market (tide rising) or Bear Market (tide receding).
    *   *Example:* Bitcoin from 2010 to 2017 was a monstrous rising tide. Even with 80% drops in the middle, the tide was up.
2.  **The Waves (Secondary Trend):**
    *   *Duration:* Weeks to Months (3 weeks to 3 months).
    *   *What it is:* corrections against the tide. If the tide rises, waves break on the sand and recede a bit.
    *   *Example:* In 2021, Bitcoin rose (Tide), but in May it fell 50% (Secondary correction Wave), only to rise again in November. Who panicked in the Wave lost the Tide.
3.  **The Ripples (Tertiary Trend):**
    *   *Duration:* Days or Hours (less than 3 weeks).
    *   *What it is:* Daily "noise". The foam on the sand.
    *   *Example:* The 15-minute chart going up and down frantically.

*   **The Inspector's Secret:** The Bot tries to identify the **Tide** (on Daily/Weekly chart) and uses the **Ripples** (15m/1h chart) to enter in favor of the Tide. It never surfs against the main tide.

#### LAW 3: The Primary Trend Has Three Psychological Phases
Here enters the study of the mass mind.

*   **Phase 1: Accumulation (The Cry of the Defeated)**
    *   *Scenario:* The market fell for months. Everything is destroyed. No one wants to hear about Stocks/Crypto. News is horrible ("Bitcoin Died").
    *   *Who trades:* Smart Money (Institutionals). They know the value is cheap. They buy silently, absorbing the selling of the desperate.
*   **Phase 2: Public Participation (Markup - The Party Begins)**
    *   *Scenario:* Price starts to rise. Breaks tops. Technical indicators signal buy.
    *   *Who trades:* Trend Followers and professional technical traders. It's the longest and most profitable phase. Where easy money is made.
*   **Phase 3: Distribution (The Euphoria of Fools)**
    *   *Scenario:* Price rose too much. Headlines on CNN. Your uncle at the BBQ asks how to open a Binance account. Uber drivers talk stocks.
    *   *Who trades:* The Masses (Retail). They enter thinking they will get rich quick.
    *   *What happens:* Smart Money, who bought in Phase 1 at $10, sells now for $100 to the Masses. Smart Money exits. The Masses are left holding the bag at the top.

#### LAW 4: Indices Must Confirm Each Other
Dow looked at Rails and Industrials.
*   **Crypto Version:** If Bitcoin (The King) breaks an all-time high, but Ethereum (The Queen) and Altcoins do not follow and show weakness, Bitcoin's breakout is suspicious. Probably just manipulation to seek liquidity. We seek confirmation together.

#### LAW 5: Volume Must Confirm the Trend
Volume is the fuel. Price is the car.
*   **Car going up hill (Uptrend):** Needs A LOT of gas (High Volume).
*   **Car going down hill (Correction):** Can go in neutral (Low Volume).
*   **Warning Sign (Divergence):** If price is rising (making new highs), but volume is decreasing with each high, it means the "tank is drying up". The car will stop and roll back. The Inspector loves to trade this failure.

#### LAW 6: The Trend Continues Until Proven Otherwise
Inertia is powerful. A train in motion tends to stay in motion.
*   **The Fatal Error:** Trying to guess the top ("It rose too much, it has to fall").
*   **The Reality:** The market can rise much more than your logic allows (irrational exuberance).
*   **The Rule:** We only believe in reversal when the chart makes a **Lower High followed by a Lower Low**. Until then, any drop is just a "Secondary Wave" (buy opportunity).

## 2. Elliott Waves: The GPS of Chaos
Ralph Nelson Elliott looked at charts and saw fractals. He realized human emotion follows a pattern of 5 steps forward and 3 back. Always.

### The Full Cycle (5-3)
Imagine a party.

*   **Wave 1 (Shy Optimism):** Party starts. Price rises a bit from the bottom. Most think it's a "dead cat bounce".
*   **Wave 2 (Reality Test):** Someone spills a drink. Price falls hard, almost to the start. If it holds the Wave 1 bottom, the setup is armed. (Inspector's favorite entry).
*   **Wave 3 (The Big Euphoria):** The party pumps. Price explodes vertically. Fundamentals improve. Volume is max. Usually the biggest wave.
*   **Wave 4 (Partial Hangover):** People get tired. Some take profit. Price goes sideways or drops slightly. A trap for the impatient.
*   **Wave 5 (End of Night):** Last gasp. Loud music, but people are leaving (Volume drops). Price makes new high, but indicators (RSI) show weakness. **Divergence**.

Then comes correction (A-B-C):
*   **Wave A:** Police arrive.
*   **Wave B:** Drunk guy says it's fine (traps bulls).
*   **Wave C:** Party over. Panic. Back to Accumulation.

## 3. The Wyckoff Method: Unmasking the Villain
Richard Wyckoff created a fictional character to explain the market: the **"Composite Man"**.
Imagine the whole market is controlled by a single super-intelligent villain.

*   He sits backstage manipulating price to confuse you.
*   When he wants to buy, he makes price look terrible so you sell cheap to him.
*   When he wants to sell, he makes price look beautiful so you buy expensive from him.

### The Wyckoff Cycle
1.  **Accumulation:** The Villain fills his bags in a sideways range at the bottom.
    *   **The Spring:** He pushes price BELOW support to trigger retail stop losses. He buys those stops. Then pumps it.
2.  **Markup:** He lets price fly.
3.  **Distribution:** He sells his bags in a sideways range at the top.
    *   **UTAD:** He pushes price ABOVE resistance to trap breakout traders. Then dumps it.
4.  **Markdown:** He shorts the market to hell.
